Belgian "Liege" Waffles

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Yield: 15-20 3-inch waffles

Description:

These waffles are smaller and sweeter than normal waffles, and can be eaten on their own or with a topping of your choice.

Ingredients

  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons light brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup warm milk (100 degrees F)
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (melted)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups pearl sugar

Directions

  1. Microwave the milk in a 2 cup pyrex.
  2. Whisk in the sugar
  3. Sprinkle in the yeast and whisk
  4. Allow the yeast to foam for 5-10 minutes
  5. Meanwhile, combine salt and flour in a large bowl. Make a well in the center
  6. Melt the butter in a 4 cup pyrex.
  7. Add the eggs and vanilla to the butter. Whisk to combine.
  8. Whisking constantly, add the yeast mixture to the butter.
  9. Add the butter and yeast mixture to the flour well, mixing flour in slowly as you stream in the liquid. Dough should be fairly smooth
  10. Proof at about 90 degrees F for around 30 minutes. Dough should double in size
  11. Once proofed, fold in the pearl sugar.
  12. Cook the dough, 1/4 cup scoop at a time, in a waffle iron set to medium heat for about 3 minutes. Waffles should reach a dark-ish golden brown color.

Notes

  • This recipe will make your waffle iron very dirty.
